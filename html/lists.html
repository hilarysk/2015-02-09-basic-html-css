HTML > Lists

Here's a bunch of reasons we love making lists, according to an NPR article:

  1. Lists bring order to chaos.
  2. Lists help us remember things.
  3. Most lists are finite.
  4. Lists can be meaningful.
  5. Lists can be as long or as short as necessary.
  6. Making lists can help make you famous.
  7. The word "list" can be tracked back to William Shakespeare.
  8. Lists relieve stress and focus the mind.
  9. Lists can force people to say revealing things.
  10. Lists can keep us from procrastinating.

So let's make some of our own!

We're just going to look at two basic list types - bulleted and numbered - but there's lots of options, which you can explore here, among other places.

Bulleted list

Example:

  • Item 1
  • Item 2
  • Item 3

Code:

<ul>
<li>Item 1</li>
<li>Item 2</li>
<li>Item 3</li>
</ul>

Numbered list

Example:

  1. Item 1
  2. Item 2
  3. Item 3

Blink and you'll miss the difference; "ul" stands for "unordered list," while "ol" refers to - you guessed it - an "ordered list." Incremental numbers are the default, but you could also use letters, Roman numerals, etc.

<ol>
<li>Item 1</li>
<li>Item 2</li>
<li>Item 3</li>
</ol>
