HTML > Images

I actually think inserting images is one of the easiest aspects of HTML, though creating captions was pretty tricky without using tables. There's a new tag in HTML5, <figcaption>, that seems promising, though my experience with HTML5 is limited.

To insert an image, use the filename path or a link:

<img src="http://photos-g.ak.instagram.com/hphotos-ak-xaf1/t51.2885-15/10956561_425987300901454_191862438_n.jpg">

You can also add additional features, like alternative text (which appears when the user scrolls over the picture with their mouse):

<img src="http://photos-g.ak.instagram.com/hphotos-ak-xaf1/t51.2885-15/10956561_425987300901454_191862438_n.jpg" alt="Description of photo">

Captions require wrapping the image in <figcaption> tags:

<figure> <img src="http://photos-g.ak.instagram.com/hphotos-ak-xaf1/t51.2885-15/10956561_425987300901454_191862438_n.jpg" alt="Description of photo"> <figcaption>Caption goes here</figcaption> </figure>

As for formatting, this again is something that probably belongs in your CSS, but can also be done on an image-by-image basis.

<figure style="width: 300px; height: 300px; vertical-align: middle; float: right; padding-bottom: 5px; padding-top: 0px; padding-left: 10px; padding-right:10px"> <img src="http://photos-g.ak.instagram.com/hphotos-ak-xaf1/t51.2885-15/10956561_425987300901454_191862438_n.jpg" alt="Description of photo" style="width: 300px; height: 300px; vertical-align: middle; float: right; padding-bottom: 5px; padding-top: 0px; padding-left: 10px; padding-right:10px""> <figcaption>Caption goes here</figcaption> </figure>

As you can see, there's a lot of different options you could include in your <style> section, but for the most part, this should go in CSS. Given the code above, we get this image:
