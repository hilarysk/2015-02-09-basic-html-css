HTML > Headers and Paragraphs

Headers

The header is not to be confused with the head. The header "represents a group of introductory or navigational aids" (source). It often contains the page's heading, logo, maybe a table of contents or search contents, etc.

Paragraphs

It's pretty simple: <p> to begin a paragraph and </p> to end it. You can also insert paragraph-specific style attributes, but if you want those attributes to apply to all paragaphs on a page, better to stick those attributes in the <body> tag or <div> tag. Likewise, if you want those attributes to apply to more or less all paragraphs on your site, incorporate the formatting into your CSS.
